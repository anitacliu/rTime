Mental Health

Being stuck at home drives us all a little stir-crazy. The inability to spend time with friends, have entertainment, and see anyone else but those you live with hurts us all. There are many studies pointing to the negative effects that a quarantine put in place to protect us caused on our mental health.

We know that fighting mental health issues is a very difficult process which, unfortuantely, has been worsened by the well-intended stay-at-home orders. That's why we at rTime have curated a collection of accessible resources to help you get your mental health, stress, anxiety, and more under control.

Stop Being So Hard on Yourself

It is easy to fall into a pattern of negligence during this pandemic; with so many tragedies happening around the world your own problems seem to be the least pressing. You might be aware of your growing restlessness at this time, of the pressure to keep your grades/work productivity high, of the anxieties that are pushing you to work extra hard to keep your job, or even the troubles of having lost your job and trying to stay afloat. It is easy right now to feel as though the stress is piling up and you have to strive for perfection. However, as Shauna Shapiro, PhD, and many other scientists communicate, research has continually shown that self-compassion and common humanity during times of stress can allow for more room for growth and productivity. Take some time to become aware of both the issues surrounding you in the world and in your own brain.
